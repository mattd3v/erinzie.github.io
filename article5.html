Your Baby Shower Essentials On a Budget

A baby shower is such a beautiful event to celebrate your soon to be child, and I feel that everyone deserves to have it go just how they dreamed of! So today I’m going to be going over how to have a flawless baby shower on a budget.

I’m about to offer you a variety of options to your baby shower, this in total can cost you anywhere from $40 to $100 for the whole thing, depending on where you buy from. Each picture links to Amazon (if you see something you like, I tried to find the most affordable options) however if you want the cheaper alternatives I highly suggest checking out your local dollar store. Almost all of these items can be bought at the dollar store and with a little diy work, your baby shower can be beautiful at minimal costs!

Baby Shower Decorations

  1. Starting with a tablecloth -- you can transform your dining room table to a cute banquet hall setting. Every Dollar Tree I’ve ever been to carries disposable table cloths, but if your town doesn’t have this store, check out your nearest Walmart as they always have very competitive prices. You can get blue or pink, or even white if you want to decorate with pops of color instead.
  2. Fabric Tulle -- Tulle is a quick and way to quickly dress up the area, bunching it up around the edges transforms your plain blue/pink table to something that looks like you put thought into it! If you can’t find affordable tulle you can always buy confetti and scatter it around your table, either one can add some texture and fun to your party’s focal points.
  3. Flowers -- Whether you get white, pink, or blue flowers this is sure to add some class to your baby shower. Real flowers can be pricey and potentially uncomfortable to pregnant women with heightened allergies. Try some fake flowers from the dollar store and put them around the table, or scatter around rose petals!
  4. Streamers -- This one is easy! Blue/pink and white streamers can quickly make your house look very festive for very cheap! Here on Amazon they are a little pricey unless you’re getting the color pink which is on sale right now for just $2. However if you want blue or white I’m sure your local stores will have it for just a dollar or 2.
  5. Balloons -- There are so many options when it comes to balloons, but some of the really nice choices out there can cost a pretty penny. If you want to keep it simple, your local dollar store or party store should be able to get you helium balloons at just a few cents each (and cute balloon weights can be found for just a dollar each as well), or you can buy a pack of balloons and blow it up to make your own arch and garland! This process can be quite timely however so if you want to go the easier route on this one and get a kit I don’t blame you!
  6. Photo Corner -- This could be a variety of things, a backdrop with a picture and the words “It’s a boy!”, tinsel foil curtains, a simple blue/pink background, or a part of the house with extra balloons. If you’re nearing the end of your budget, try checking out your stores for clearance curtains or shower curtains. These can usually already be just a few dollars for a solid colored curtain, but if it’s more than a few dollars, try these tinsel curtains they’re cute and bold for just $12.
  7. Food -- Food does not necessarily need to be a focal point of your shower, but if you’re excited to have things like bright pink/blue cupcakes this can be done at an affordable price! Most grocery stores will have 6-12 packs of muffins or cupcakes with white icing, for something a little more fun you can buy a tub of icing and food coloring (be sure to only add a little at a time and mix so the color isn’t too dark, especially if using red food coloring to make pink icing). This trick can make anything look just right for the baby shower vibe you want. Try rice crispy treats dipped halfway into blue icing and left to harden, or cake pops dipped into pink icing. For drinks try serving pink lemonade or blue powerade in baby bottles or a traditional punch bowl. And most importantly there’s the cake! You can go all out and get a beautiful but pricey cake if you’d like, or you can try making this onesie cake, it looks adorable and making it from scratch isn’t hard if you have some basic baking experience!

Baby Shower Games

  1. Don't Say Baby -- Everyone knows this game for a reason, it's one of the easiest games you can play and the kids always love it. Give each of your guests a pin to wear on his/her shirt, once all of your guests have arrived, tell everyone that they can't say 'baby' for the duration of the shower (or until it's time to open presents). If anyone hears someone else saying the word which shall not be named, they can steal the rule breaker's pin. At the end of the game, the person with the most pins wins a gift!
  2. Baby Sketch Artists -- This fun game entails passing out paper plates & markers to all of your guests and having them draw a picture of the baby while wearing the plate on their head! This is sure to get the party going as everyone can laugh over their drawing fails, the best sketch wins!
  3. Dirty Diapers -- No one is a fan of poopy diapers, but what if the gooey center was actually candy? Place several types of melted chocolate candies in newborn-sized diapers, make sure you have many types and consistencies represented: milky, creamy, nutty, caramel-filled, etc. Pass around the diapers and have each person sniff and lick the 'poo' and guess which brand of candy bar is in each diaper. The person with the best nose and strongest stomach wins!
  4. Bottles Up! -- The game can be fun for all ages, whether you're planning an adult shower or a family party. Fill baby bottles with a drink of your choice: beer or wine for the grown-ups' party and juice for the kids. Give everyone a bottle and on go they must suck the bottles dry as quickly as they can. The first person to down their bottle wins!
  5. Diaper Derby -- Divide your guests into teams and give each team a roll of toilet paper. Give the teams 1 minute to wrap a team member up in a mock diaper. The team with the most creatively diapered 'baby' wins. There's something so funny about grown adults in quickly assembled toilet paper diapers!
  6. Ice Ice Baby -- The night before your shower, freeze a bunch of ice cubes with Mini Plastic Babies in the middle (you can find these at any craft store). When guests arrive, drop an ice cube into a cup of water and present it to each person. As time goes by, the ice begins to melt and the baby is closer and closer to its due date! Whoever has a baby first, wins. You'll have a roomful of amusingly anxious faux parents-to-be with this game.
  7. Diaper Raffle -- It's recommended to change a baby's diaper 10 times a day in the early weeks and months, that's at least 70 diapers a week! This simple game is such an perfect gift for the parents-to-be. Include a note with your baby shower invitations letting guests know that you'll be raffling off an amazing surprise prize at the baby shower. All they have to do to get a raffle ticket is bring a package of diapers with them and the more diapers they bring, the more raffle tickets they get! On the day of the shower, diapers will be crammed into every nook and cranny. Long after the party, the parents will still be thankful with each diaper they use, just be sure a variety of sizes is brought as you don't want hundreds of newborn diapers that the little one will grow out of before using!
